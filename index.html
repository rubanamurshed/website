What do I do?

I conduct behavioural neuroscience research, exploring brain-behaviour relationships through neuroimaging and cognitive neuroscience. My work focuses on finding neural mechanisms underlying psychiatric conditions and creating insights that drive meaningful change.

Through my research projects at the Douglas Research Centre, I collaborate with experts across disciplines — from mouse behaviour to 7T fMRI in humans — to better understand how the brain works and how it can break down.
